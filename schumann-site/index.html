What is the Schumann Resonance?

The Schumann resonance is a set of electromagnetic resonances in the cavity between Earth's surface and the ionosphere. Predicted by physicist Winfried Otto Schumann in 1952, these standing waves are excited by lightning discharges — over 2,000 active thunderstorms occur at any given moment around the planet.

The fundamental frequency sits at 7.83 Hz, with harmonics at 14.3, 20.8, 27.3, and 33.8 Hz. These frequencies are used in atmospheric research, space weather monitoring, and geophysical studies.
